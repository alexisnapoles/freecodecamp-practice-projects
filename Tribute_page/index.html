Princess Elizabeth Charlotte, wife of Philippe I, Duke of Orléans, proclaimed, “Cats are the most entrancing animals in the world.”

In the 1880s, a cat in the Arizona territory sold for $10—a huge sum at the time—and in Alaska, felines were worth literally their weight in gold.

Pop Art pioneer Andy Warhol owned up to 25 cats at a time, all of them Siamese and all, except one, named Sam.

Napoléon Bonaparte hated cats, once stating, “There are two kinds of fidelity, that of dogs and that of cats.”

By the early 19th century, cats were standard equipment in U.S. Army commissary storehouses.

Cardinal Richelieu, chief minister of France's Louis XIII, was also fond of cats, preferring to have at least a dozen surrounding him at all times.
